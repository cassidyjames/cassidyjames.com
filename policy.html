Dan on Public Policy

It's my belief that we can only solve our biggest problems by identifying and adressing their root causes. I've chosen to highlight some of the things below as what I believe to be big solutions for many of the problems we face in the US today.

Take the iSideWith Quiz

Economy

Implement Universal Basic Income

Job automation is inevitable, but more than that job automation is good for society. We should strive for a future where people are no longer forced to find work in order to survive. UBI allows for an economy where the elimination of jobs doesn't mean starvation and homelessness.

Hear More about Basic Income

Abolish Means-Tested Welfare

Current state assistance programs that rely on means testing create a situation where finding work may result in a net loss of income. Means testing also implies overhead to determine eligibility. This means that people participating in these programs are disincentivized to find work and we pay extra to keep it that way.

Abolish Minimum Wage

Economists say that raising the minimum wage is either harmful or has no effect. UBI would allow us to abolish the minimum wage, creating a free-er market by lowering costs of production.

Energy

Produce Energy Domestically

The price of energy affects the price of everything. When gas prices go up, so do bread prices. Entangling energy and foreign policy allows the US economy to be held hostage by outside interests. Domestic energy eliminates the need to pursue foreign conflicts in order to secure energy prices.

Produce Energy Cleanly

Energy production needs to be clean, not just for the air we breath but also for the water we drink. Recent disasters in Flint, Michigan and Los Angeles, California only serve to highlight that our current means of energy production are unsafe and cause tangible public harm in addition to contributing to adverse climate change.

Stop Climate Change

A transition from fossil fuels is necessary to combat the largest global security issue of our time: climate change. The adverse effects of climate change have already lead to civil unrest in the Middle East. If we do nothing by 2100, Over 1 Million US citizens could be forced to relocate due to rising sea levels.

Health

Implement Single Payer Healthcare

The US spends more money per capita on healthcare than other leading countries and many of the people who need healthcare assistance most still don't qualify. We need to implement a universal, single payer healthcare system to ensure that all Americans have access to healthcare and to cut administrative costs.
